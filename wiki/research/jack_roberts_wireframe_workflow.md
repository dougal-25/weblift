# Research: Jack Roberts — wireframe-first website workflow with Claude Code

**Source:** [YouTube — "build multi-page websites with wireframes that sell for thousands"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zTuV3chQCus) (Jack Roberts)
**Ingested:** 2026-05-29 · via `docs/tools/transcriber`
**Why it's here:** This is a soup-to-nuts playbook for the *exact* thing Web Lift sells — done-for-you multi-page small-business sites. The core thesis ("substance first, then style") and the research → wireframe → build pipeline directly inform our [delivery process](delivery_process.md).

---

## The one big idea

> "99% of AI-built sites are flashy front ends nobody buys. It's a Ferrari with no engine."

Pretty front-ends fail because they have **no structure and no purpose**. The fix is to do the thinking *before* the styling: research what wins in the niche → build a wireframe/sitemap → *then* let the AI make it beautiful. **Substance first, then style.**

## The 5 questions to ask before building anything

The step "everybody misses." Ask these about the *visitor*, not the business:

1. **Who is the website for?** (the visitor — e.g. "busy homeowners with a stained driveway", not "a driveway company")
2. **What one action do you want them to take?** (the single primary CTA — e.g. "get a free quote")
3. **What objections does the visitor have?**
4. **What is the vibe?**
5. **Do you have existing brand assets to use?**

## The workflow (research → wireframe → build → animate → host)

1. **Research the niche** — find the top 5 performers (judged by reviews, SEO) and answer the 5 questions from what *works*. (He uses Firecrawl MCP + Claude to scrape + summarise; the principle matters more than the tool.)
2. **Sitemap + wireframe before code** — don't jump straight to a one-page site. Decide every page (home, services, service detail, gallery, about, contact) and the section order *first*. He uses **Relume** to generate sitemap → wireframe → style guide, then export.
3. **Build with Claude Code** — wire the exported structure into a real multi-page site, matched to the sitemap.
4. **Generate real imagery** — replace placeholders with on-brand images (he uses Nano Banana 2 via Kie API; before/after shots + a transition video via Higgsfield).
5. **Add one signature animation** — e.g. a scroll-driven before/after reveal. One "wow" moment, not slop everywhere.
6. **Host** — GitHub repo → Vercel, one route per page.

## What's directly useful for Web Lift

- **Adopt the 5 questions as our intake script.** They map cleanly onto our enquiry/audit step and stop us building pretty-but-purposeless pages.
- **Wireframe/sitemap sign-off before build** — sell the *structure* first; it's what justifies the flat fee vs. a £20 Wix template. Consider showing clients the sitemap as a deliverable.
- **"Substance first, then style"** is a sharper articulation of our own pitch — reusable copy for the landing page and outreach.
- **The before/after slider** (already a [landing-page decision](landing_page.md)) is exactly the kind of signature animation he advocates — we're aligned.
- **Niche research = an unfair advantage.** Studying the top 5 performers per trade (plumbers, cafes, etc.) before quoting is a repeatable edge.
