# Research — Niche selection for cold-call website sales

**Date:** 2026-06-04
**Question:** Which UK local-business niche should Web Lift cold-call to sell £750–£1,500 redesigns?
**Outcome:** Pivot from garages → **high-ticket home-improvement trades**.

## The one filter that decides it: *do they already buy leads?*

The sales motion is cold-calling owners and offering a demonstrable before/after redesign. The
single best predictor of whether that converts is whether the owner **already pays for leads** —
because then "leads = money" is a belief you don't have to install; you're just offering a cheaper
source of them.

- **Garages fail this test.** Independent auto-repair/MOT garages run on **locality +
  word-of-mouth + repeat** (83% of customers pick a garage within 30 mins of home). There's no
  Checkatrade-for-garages they buy into. So a website pitch has to sell the *belief* before the
  product — double the friction. This is why the garage outreach produced conversations but **no
  conversions**: they were polite, not in pain. (Note: car *dealerships* do buy leads — don't
  confuse them with repair garages.)
- **High-ticket trades pass it emphatically.** They already pay £600–£1,500/yr to Checkatrade
  plus £11–£100 *per lead* on Bark / MyBuilder / Rated People. One kitchen (£3–15k) or extension
  (£10k+) job pays for a £950 site **3–15× over** — a true statement you can make on the phone.

## Decision: primary niche = home-improvement trades

Kitchen & bathroom fitters, extension/loft builders, landscapers & driveway companies, roofers.
Three independent research angles all point here:

| Axis | Why trades win |
|---|---|
| **Lead-buying mindset** | Already pay for leads (see above). ROI pitch is pre-sold. |
| **Reachability (cold call)** | Owner-operated, **no gatekeeper** — the builder answers his own phone. (Catch him early/lunch/after-5; he's on-site otherwise.) |
| **Receptiveness** | ~67% rely on word-of-mouth and *know* it's a ceiling; they feel the cashflow dips. Sceptical of cold-call BS, so lead with the demonstrable before/after. |
| **Retooling cost** | Lowest-friction pivot from garages — same build pattern (services + photo gallery + reviews + quote form). Garage mockup components transfer almost directly. |

**Honest caveat:** trades are *not* virgin territory — specialist trade-web agencies exist
(Builder Web, Welch Marketing). But the market is enormous (220k–360k UK construction/trade
firms), digitally immature, with no per-town incumbent. Web Lift wins on local hustle, price,
speed, and show-don't-tell — not on being the only option.

## Strong second choice: wedding suppliers

Wedding photographers / florists / caterers — pure solo operators, no gatekeeper, the website
*is* the shopfront, intensely visual. One catch: **time calls to their off-season** (avoid the
Nov–Feb engagement-season crunch when they're slammed).

## Avoid (for a cold-call motion)

- **Aesthetic clinics, accountants, solicitors** — great budgets, but **gatekept by
  reception/PA**. Wrong channel for cold-calling the owner. (Clinics also read £950 as "too cheap
  to be serious".)
- **Dentists, estate agents** — over-served by niche agencies; need PMS/listing integrations we
  can't undercut at our price point.
- **Restaurants, beauty salons** — reachable but low per-customer value + price-sensitive. Weak
  ROI story.

## ⚠️ Compliance — build into the scraping pipeline from day one

UK B2B live cold calls are governed by **PECR** (enforced by the ICO). Before dialling:

- **Screen every number against BOTH TPS and CTPS.** Limited companies/LLPs sit on CTPS; **sole
  traders & most partnerships sit on the *consumer* TPS register** — and most solo trades and
  wedding suppliers are sole traders, so TPS matters more than people expect. Re-screen ~every 28
  days.
- **On every call:** state your business name, don't withhold your number.
- **Penalty:** ICO fines up to £500k. (The Data (Use and Access) Act became law 19 Jun 2025;
  ICO guidance is under review — re-check before scaling.)

## Validation caveat

Reachability/receptiveness rankings are synthesised from marketing-industry commentary and
sector behaviour, not a single "owner answer-rate" survey, and there is no public dataset of
"% without a website *by sector*" (the reliable figure is national: 32% of UK businesses, 35% of
sole traders, have no website). The trades conclusion is well-supported directionally — **validate
with a small test batch of calls before committing the full pipeline.**
