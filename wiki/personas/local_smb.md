# Persona — The Local SMB Owner

## Who
"Mark", 48, runs a plumbing business in South London. Or "Sofia", 35, owns a cafe. Or a gym, a dentist, an electrician, a hair salon. Owner-operator. Excellent at the actual trade. Not a "computer person" and proud of it.

## Their current website situation
- A site built years ago by a relative, a cheap freelancer, or a Wix free trial — then abandoned.
- Looks dated. Breaks on a phone (where 70%+ of their customers actually look).
- Slow to load. Hard to find the phone number. No clear "book" or "get a quote" button.
- They haven't touched it in years because they don't know how and don't have the login.

## What they feel
- **Embarrassed** — they know it looks bad and assume customers judge them for it.
- **Stuck** — they know they're losing business but fixing it feels like a big, scary, expensive project.
- **Burned or wary** — either been quoted thousands by an agency, or got ghosted by a cheap freelancer.
- **Time-poor** — every hour on "the website" is an hour not running the business.

## What they actually want
- A site that looks professional and works on a phone.
- To be found on Google when someone searches "[trade] near me".
- An easy way for customers to call, book, or get a quote.
- To **not have to think about it**. Hand it over, get it sorted, get back to work.
- A price they can say yes to without a board meeting.

## What they DON'T want
- Jargon ("responsive CMS headless SEO funnel").
- To make a hundred decisions or learn any software.
- An open-ended bill.
- A months-long project with weekly calls.

## How Web Lift speaks to them
- "Your website's letting you down. We'll fix it in days, for one fixed price."
- Show a before/after. They get it instantly.
- One price. One short conversation. Done.

## Where they are
- Searching Google. Asking in local Facebook groups / business networks. Word of mouth.
- They are NOT on Product Hunt, Twitter, or Hacker News. Reach them where local trades actually are. See [outreach plan](outreach.md).
